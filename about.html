About Matt

I'm Matt Bedford, a woodworker based in the UK. I build furniture, boxes, lamps, little wooden houses, and whatever else the timber asks to become. Everything is made by hand in my small workshop.

By day I'm a software developer. Woodwork is the antidote — something slow, physical, and real. No screens, no deploys, just sharp tools and good grain.

I work mostly with reclaimed and locally sourced hardwoods: oak, ash, cherry, walnut. Each piece is hand-finished with oils and waxes rather than spray lacquers. It takes longer, but you can feel the difference.

The philosophy

No table saws. No routers. No power sanders. Apart from a couple of drills, everything in the workshop runs on effort and patience. That's a deliberate choice, not a limitation.

I believe in joinery over fixings — dovetails, mortise and tenon, finger joints, housing joints. Proper joints that hold because the wood itself wants to stay together, not because a screw is forcing it. You won't find nails in my work. If the joinery is right, you don't need them.

Truthfully, all I need is a chisel, a hand plane and a hammer. Everything else is extraneous.
